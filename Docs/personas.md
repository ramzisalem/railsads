Personas are defined based on behavior, goals, and decision-making, not demographics.
We focus on how users use the product, not who they are.

🎯 Persona 1 — Performance Marketer

Overview
Primary user focused on scaling ads and performance

Goal
Generate high-performing ad creatives quickly and at scale

Pain Points
Needs constant new creatives
Testing takes too long
Creative fatigue
Manual iteration is slow

Behavior
Iterates frequently
Tests multiple hooks and angles
Prefers speed over perfection
Works with structured frameworks (angles, ICPs, hooks)

Needs
Fast generation
Multiple variations
Easy iteration
Clear structure (hooks, angles, formats)

What they value
Speed
Volume
Control
Clarity

Risk
Will leave if the tool feels slow or generic

🎯 Persona 2 — Founder / Operator

Overview
Non-expert user creating ads without deep marketing knowledge

Goal
Create effective ads without needing expertise

Pain Points
Doesn’t know what makes a good ad
Overwhelmed by choices
Lacks structured thinking (angles, ICPs)

Behavior
Relies on system guidance
Follows recommendations
Iterates less frequently

Needs
Clear direction
Simple workflows
Guided decisions
Confidence in output

What they value
Clarity
Simplicity
Guidance
Trust

Risk
Will drop if the product feels complex or unclear

🎯 Persona 3 — Creative Strategist (Optional)

Overview
User focused on exploring creative angles and ideas deeply

Goal
Discover new creative directions and refine messaging

Pain Points
Needs inspiration
Wants deeper exploration
Generic outputs are not useful

Behavior
Explores multiple angles
Iterates conceptually
Uses the tool for thinking and ideation

Needs
Flexible iteration
Diverse outputs
Creative depth

What they value
Quality
Originality
Exploration

Risk
Will leave if outputs feel repetitive or shallow

🧾 Summary
Personas are defined by behavior:

1. Performance Marketer → speed + volume
2. Founder → clarity + guidance
3. Strategist → depth + exploration

The product must balance all three without becoming complex
